Research Overview: Multi-label Classification

Multi-label classification is a task that involves classifying data points that can be assigned multiple labels simultaneously. Unlike simple classification problems with a single label, multi-label classification associates multiple labels with each data point. For example, in multi-label image classification, an image may represent different objects or attributes simultaneously, and thus multiple labels may be assigned. For instance, an image of a dog could have both "dog" and "walking" labels. Multi-label classification is utilized in various application domains. In text classification, it is used when a document relates to multiple topics. Similarly, in music and video classification, it helps predict labels associated with multiple genres or attributes.
